Entities in HTML are special codes used to represent characters that either have special meaning in HTML syntax or are not readily available on a keyboard. 
Generally used for:
  Display reserved characters (like <, >, &, etc.).
  Represent invisible or non-breaking spaces.
  Display special symbols or special characters.

Entities begin with an ampersand (&) and end with a semicolon (;).

Common entities used in HTML are listed below:
&nbps; - used for adding extra spaces (since HTML wraps sequence of spaces to single space)
&amp; - For symbol '&' 
&lt -  For symbol '<'
&gt - Fo symbol '>'
&quot; -  For quotation mark 
&apos; - For apostrophe
